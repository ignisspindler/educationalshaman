About

Guiding Transformation Through the Art of Learning

I'm Eugene Geis—physicist, ordained minister, martial artist, and educator. I hold dual PhDs: the first in Experimental Nuclear Physics, the second in Statistical Psychometric Methods, earned summa cum laude. The arc between them is part of the story. I've also been meditating since I was fifteen. That combination isn't accidental. It's the foundation of everything I do here.

Early in my teaching career I coached a wrestling team that had stopped believing it could win. We didn't train harder. We shifted how they understood themselves as learners and competitors. They became district champions. That moment showed me something I've spent decades refining: transformation follows understanding, not effort alone.

Real learning moves in two directions at once. Outward, into new skills, fields, and ways of making your mark on the world. Inward, toward a clearer sense of who you are and what you're here to do. As your learning guide I help you honor both. Through a proven sequence of seven questions, we find what made your best learning feel alive, and design pathways that keep that spirit at the center of wherever you want to go next.

I've also coached students to Best Delegation honors at Harvard MUN and NAIMUN, authored MUN-E (a youth leadership manual), won a gold medal at the NYC U.S. Open in Tai Chi push hands, and performed as a singer and comedian—because some truths land better as punchlines.

The Method

Seven Questions That
Transform How You Learn

This isn't about learning faster or more efficiently. It's about learning in a way that awakens something in you. Each question is a door, outward into new capability, and inward toward deeper self-knowledge.

01

What is the most positive or inspiring learning experience you've ever had?

We begin at the source. What moment in your past made learning feel magical, important, real? This is your north star, a reference point for what's possible.

02

Is there anything you feel drawn to learn right now?

There's a reason you're here. Whatever the pull is toward, a skill, a field, a practice, or a new paradigm, it points toward growth.

03

What was it about that earlier learning experience that made it feel alive for you?

Here's where we get specific. Was it the teacher? The challenge level? The sense of relevance? The freedom to explore? We identify the ingredients.

04

How could you bring more of that same energy into learning this new thing?

Now we design. How do we adapt what worked before to this new context? What needs to change? What needs to stay the same?

05

If you really gave yourself to this new learning journey, who might you become through it?

Beyond the subject itself: who does learning this make you? What does it stir in you spiritually, not just professionally? This is the transformational question, the one that turns a course into a calling.

06

Would you like help designing a path that keeps that sense of inspiration alive?

Practical design phase. We create a learning architecture that honors your energy, not just your calendar.

07

And if learning could feel this alive more often, what else would you want to explore?

Final step: expansion. Once you've tasted transformation, what's next? This question opens the door to ongoing growth.
